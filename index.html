What device are you using?

Go to Settings and type update in the search bar.

Press the option for System update, Check for updates, or something similar.

Go to Settings -> General -> Software Update.

It's best if you have your phone plugged in and connected to Wi-Fi.

Encrypt your phone

Most newer phones enable this by default. To make sure, go to Settings and type encrypt into the search bar. Choose an option like Encrypt phone and make sure it is labeled On, Encrypted, or something similar. If not, tap the Encrypt phone option to begin the process. This could take an hour or more and you should keep your phone plugged in until it's finished.

Disable biometrics

Go to Settings -> Touch [or Face] ID & Passcode and enter your passcode, then toggle off iPhone Unlock.

Make sure your PIN or password is a strong one.

Go to Settings -> Security and choose Screen lock. Select PIN or Password and enter a strong one.

Toggle off or disable any fingerprint or facial recognition features.

For Google Pixels, in Security, go to Pixel Imprint and press the delete icon next to each fingerprint to delete them, which disables fingerprint access.

Improve your lock

Press the settings icon next to Screen lock. Turn Power button instantly locks to on.

Return to the Security menu and select Smart lock. Select each smart lock option and make sure it's disabled on the next screen.

You can quickly access your camera even when your phone is locked by swiping left on the lock screen. You can access your camera from the lock screen by quickly pressing the power button twice.

Communication

Download the Signal app for encrypted texts and phone calls.

Texts sent between iMessage users and FaceTime calls are end-to-end encrypted, and messages stored on your iPhone are encrypted as well.

If you have contacts who don't use an iPhone, you both can download the Signal app for encrypted texts and phone calls.

Be aware that your contacts need to be using Signal too for encryption to work.

Web traffic

Download the 1.1.1.1 app from Cloudflare to automatically encrypt all your internet traffic and DNS requests by default.

It's up to you to decide if taking measures to prevent your location from being tracked is appropriate for your situation.

Disable Wi-Fi and Bluetooth

Go to Settings -> Wi-Fi and toggle Wi-Fi off.

Go to Settings -> Bluetooth and toggle Bluetooth off.

Turning these off in the Control Center is not effective.

Go to Settings and open your Network settings, then toggle Wi-Fi and Bluetooth off. If Bluetooth is not present, search bluetooth in Settings.

In Settings, search scanning and toggle off both Wi-Fi and Bluetooth scanning.

Disable GPS

Go to Settings -> Privacy -> Location Services and toggle Location Services off.

Go to Settings -> Location, or search location, and toggle it off.

If Airplane Mode is enabled, it's probably safe to turn on your location so you can see where you are on a navigation app.

Guard your location

Law enforcement agencies can use devices often called Stingrays which mimic cell towers and can intercept calls, texts, and internet traffic sent from your phone.

Your phone also broadcasts a unique identifier called an IMSI to cell towers that can be used to track your movements and prove that you attended a protest.

The only way to positively prevent your location from being tracked is to keep your phone off or leave it at home. However, enabling Airplane Mode before you arrive at the protest, leaving it enabled the entire time you're there, and disabling it only after you leave may make it harder to prove you were there.

You can also be identified by automatic license plate readers or facial recognition systems. For that reason, consider not driving your car to a protest if possible, and keep your face covered with glasses, a face bandana, and a head covering near the area of the protest. Be aware of any features that distinguish you like distinct backpacks, tattoos, or hairstyles.
